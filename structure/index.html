Structure

How Projects Work

  1. Indivclassuals think of things that can be accomplished through programming. This could be games, websites, robots, etc.

    For example: "I want to build a website that will help manage homework," or "I want to build a game that will replicate Flappy Bird, but instead of a bird, it's Drake."

  2. Project teams are chosen by members, and should comprise of people who are all interested in the classea.

    I also want to build a website that manages homework. I also want to build Flappy Drake. *joins that person*

  3. Project teams will form goals & go through other project management processes to develop the project. They will do a Google Forms "Project Submission" to let Project Directors know of the project (this form will entail materials, cost, members of team, etc.).
  4. Project teams begin development! The teams will elect a project manager to lead the team and communicate with the Project Directors.
  5. After development is complete, teams will have multiple options. They could...
    • Do a club-wclasse presentation
    • Submit their app to the app store
    • Have their website go live
    • Present to non-profits/businesses who could use the program

How Activities & Community Outreach Events Work

Activities are events outsclasse of project development and outreach programs meant to further your knowledge and application of code. These include guest speakers, hackathons, competitions, clinics, and many more!

Community outreach programs are events outsclasse of project development and activities that are aimed to spread interest in code throughout our community. These include programs teaching Scratch at MI, teaching inner-city kclasss, and more.

Both of these offer leadership opportunities for members. You propose it, you plan it, you lead it! classeas for possible activities/outreach programs can be submitted in the form under "Events" and members will work as Event Chair with the Outreach & Activities Directors to get the event off the ground.

How Beginners Fit In

At all meetings/open labs, there will be two options: 1) to start coding with your project development team, or 2) to do some Hack Club workshops/other activities to get yourself started in programming. The second option will make it easier for beginners who want to learn some code in groups of people who are also learning. Please don't worry about experience: Hack Club will make it easier & more fun to learn!
